About DIGIT


DIGIT is a platform for Professor Shapiro to test his optical illusions. It lets the user see where they stand concerning color contrast and spatial recognition. Rather than interacte with still optical illusions, DIGIT allows the user to play with optical illusions and see how what they are seeing can change with just small adjustments.

The data gathered from this site will help with research concerning perception of color and space. It will provide a way to crowdsoure large groups of people and see how they percieve different illusions and colors.

This site was developed for American University's Computer Science capstone project by Natalie Forman in collaboration with Arthur Shapiro

Arthur Shapiro

Arthur Shapiro is a Professor of Psychology and Computer Science at American University in Washington, D.C. In 2008 he started a blog on illusions but stopped in 2009, when he was relocated to Washington, D.C, and began teaching at American University.

Since ending his previous blog, he has been involved in a number of other public outreach activities:

  • Co-editor of the forthcoming Oxford Compendium of Visual Illusions
  • Appeared on CuriosityStream’s series on visual illusions
  • Appeared on an episode of NatGeo’s Brain Games.
  • Made guest appearances on the New England Sports Network’s Baseball Lab.
  • Became a member of the U.S. Science and Engineering Festival’s Nifty-Fifty, a group of international professionals organized to promote STEM fields to U.S. secondary school students.

Natalie Forman
